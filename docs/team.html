About Me (The Project Leader)

Hello/Salaam/Kia ora. I am Usman Afzali - a research fellow at University of Canterbury. I completed my PhD in psychology and am currently the principal investigator (project leader) of the three-year New Zealand Attitudes and Values Study (NZAVS) Flourishing Project (started 2023).

Beside research work, I teach two courses, Computational Contemplative-Neuroscience and Intermediate Research Methods and Statistics, in the School of Psychology at University of Canterbury. I have supervised research and am willing to take on students.

In my spare time, I learn programming - especially I like to write R code for the courses I teach.

My CV, publications, and further information can be found on my website.

The Project Co-leaders

The co-leaders consist of senior academics and engaged community members from across New Zealand.

Professor Joesph Bulbulia

Prof Bulbulia is a professor of psychology at Victoria University of Wellington, and one of four senior managers of the NZAVS. He developed the first NZAVS studies on anti- Muslim prejudice in 2015 (with postdoc Dr John Shaver) and works with government and Muslim communities to promote acceptance. He co-directs this project, mentors Dr Afzali, promotes international outreach, and co-authors publications.

Associate Professor Kumar Yogeeswaran

A/Prof Yogeeswaran is an associate professor of psychology at University of Canterbury. A/Prof Kumar has been an active member of the NZAVS advisory board since 2014. He has published research using the NZAVS data, including several recent scientific articles relating to the terrorist attacks against the Muslim community. As a faculty member at the University of Canterbury, A/Prof Kumar will support and mentor the lead PI, Dr Afzali in the project.

Professor Chris Sibley

Prof Chris Sibley is a professor of psychology at the University of Auckland, and NZAVS founder and lead manager. Prof Sibley is among the world’s top researchers in longitudinal psychology. He manages the collected NZAVS Flourishing Study data at the basic level and will co-author publications.

Barriester Aarif Rasheed

Barrister Aarif Abdul Rasheed is the head of the Muslim advocacy group, Just Community. He has been a close and valued NZAVS collaborator since 2016. Barrister Rasheed advises the team in this project and works with the Muslim community leadership and Research Assistants, particularly in Auckland and Wellington.
